In the Celtic saga Brian, Iuchar and Iucharba, the sons of Tuireann, kill Cian MacCainte. Cian's son Lugh requests following price for his father's death:

  • Three apples from the garden in the east of the world that heal suffering and pain;
  • The skin of the pig of the king of Greece that heals all wounds and sicknesses, also when it walks through a river the water turns into wine for nine days and heals all wounds;
  • The spear Luin of the king of Persia, the tip of the spear is in a vessle of water else it would burn everything;
  • The carriage and two horses of Dobar, king of Siogair, that are the fastest in the world and can run on water;
  • The seven pigs of Easal, king of the golden pillars, that can be killed and eaten but are alive again the next day, their meat heals sicknesses and deceases;
  • The puppy Fail-Inis of the king of Ioruaidh, the cold land;
  • The spit of the women of Inis Cenn-fhinne;
  • Three calls on the hill of Miochaoin.

(Source: German edition of "Irish Myths and Legends" by Lady Gregory)
